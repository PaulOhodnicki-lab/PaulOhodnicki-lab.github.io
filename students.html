Senior Research Associates


Bo Liu


bol36@pitt.edu

Dr. Bo Liu received his dual B.S. degree in Optical Engineering from Huazhong University of Science and Technology and Mechanical Engineering from Wuhan University of Technology. He earned M.S. in electromechanical engineering from the Wuhan University of Technology and the Ph.D. in electrical and computer engineering at Virginia Tech, Blacksburg, USA. After his graduation, Dr. Liu joined US National Energy Technology Laboratory (NETL) as a Research Engineer for 4 years. He is the inventor of Distributed Temperature Sensing technique using single crystal fiber, and the primary designer of over three sets of Laser Heated Pedestal Growth system at Virginia Tech, NETL, and Pitt.

Yang-Duan Su


yas57@pitt.edu

Yang-Duan received his B.S. in Chemical Engineering from National Cheng-Kung University in Taiwan. Before he earned a M.S. in Energy Science, Technology and Policy from Carnegie Mellon Univeristy, he was an instrumentation engineer at CTCI Corp., Taiwan. He previously worked on multi-objective optimization of power inductors for EV applications. He is now researching embedded fiber optic sensors for electrical assets including inductors, transformers and EV battery systems
